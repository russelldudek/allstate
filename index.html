The operating contradictions

The job is to hold the tensions, not pretend they disappear.

A central platform earns adoption only when it protects what domain teams need while removing the failure modes no team should solve alone.

Product velocity
Enterprise assurance
Controls must be executable and reusable, not a late-stage approval queue.
Model optionality
Stable platform contracts
Teams need choice without coupling every workflow to a vendor-specific interface.
Agent autonomy
Human accountability
Authority boundaries must change with consequence, reversibility, evidence, and recovery.
Central leverage
Domain ownership
The platform owns the paved road; business and product owners retain outcome and policy authority.
Innovation pace
Unit economics
The route to scale needs cost per completed outcome, not only token or infrastructure expense.
Candidate thesis

Build the platform around an inspectable AI action contract.

The contract is not a document. It is the runtime behavior and evidence generated across four platform planes. The flight record makes those planes legible to engineers, product owners, security, risk, operations, and executives.

Access plane

One governed front door

Identity, entitlements, tenant boundaries, model gateway, routing, quotas, secrets, approved providers, and portable APIs.

Work plane

Reusable AI application primitives

Retrieval, prompt and context assembly, orchestration, tool use, agent state, deterministic services, and human checkpoints.

Assurance plane

Controls that execute with the workload

Policy-as-code, evaluation suites, red-team evidence, authorization boundaries, audit events, data handling, and lifecycle decisions.

Operations plane

Production truth

Tracing, SLOs, resilience, fallback, incident response, cost attribution, outcome telemetry, drift, ownership, and retirement.

Paved road, not tollboothDefault paths should make the safe, observable, reusable choice the fastest choice.
Authority is a runtime propertyAssist, recommend, and act require different permissions, evidence, review, and recovery.
Trace the completed outcomeMeasure the work result, adoption, trust, and unit economics - not model activity in isolation.
Every exception teaches the platformIncidents, overrides, and local workarounds should feed standards, reusable components, and stop decisions.

120-day entry hypothesis

Discover before prescribing. Prove the contract before scaling the catalog.

This plan is a hypothesis for discovery. It assumes the role is a senior technical authority working across existing platform, engineering, data, security, risk, and product structures.

Days 0-30

Read the system

  • Map current AI workloads, model-access paths, controls, owners, and incident evidence.
  • Listen to platform consumers and governance actors.
  • Identify one high-value path and one recurring friction pattern.
Days 31-60

Define the contract

  • Draft reference architecture and AI action trace schema.
  • Clarify authority tiers, evidence standards, SLO classes, and economics fields.
  • Review with engineering, data, cybersecurity, risk, product, and business owners.
Days 61-90

Prove a paved path

  • Apply the contract to one existing or near-term workload.
  • Instrument trace, evaluation, fallback, ownership, and cost per outcome.
  • Capture developer friction and governance cycle time.
Days 91-120

Scale the learning

  • Publish reusable patterns and service-catalog candidates.
  • Establish operating review and lifecycle cadence.
  • Prioritize the next platform capabilities from evidence, not novelty.
